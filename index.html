Bio

Hi! I am Ruiji Liu, a Graduate Research Assistant at Kantor Lab, CMU Robotics Institute. I am working on Intelligent robot arm manipulation with AI and autonomous navigation in field robotics under the guidance of Prof. George Kantor. I am interested in research problems related to Perception & Control for autonomous navigation and manipulation with AI on various robotic systems (UGV, UAV, & legged robots) in diverse environments. My research goal is to enable robots to replace humans in dangerous tasks like rescue or discovery in unknown environments and in tedious tasks such as transporting goods. Specifically, I am interested in research areas including CV, SLAM, path planning, & control with Robot Learning methods applied.

Previously, I received my master of science degree in mechanical engineering with a concentration in robotics and control systems at CMU, supervised by Prof. George Kantor. I worked as a research assistant in Kantor Lab, researching on lidar-Based crop-agnostic autonomous navigation. My master's thesis is "Towards Over-Canopy Autonomous Navigation: Crop-Agnostic LiDAR-Based Crop-Row Detection in Arable Fields". Before my master, I received my bachelor of science degree in mechanical engineering at Penn State University, where I was advised by Prof. Jason Moore and Prof. Alan Wagner. During my undergradue, I worked on construction robot and medical robot.
